About The Deccan Table

Preserving Flavors, Serving Traditions

Our Story

Founded in 1940 by the Ahmed family, The Deccan Table began as a small eatery in the heart of Hyderabad. What started as a humble venture to share our family recipes has blossomed into a beloved culinary institution spanning three generations.

Grandpa Abdul's secret spice blends and Grandma Fatima's meticulous cooking techniques form the foundation of every dish we serve. We've stayed true to our roots while carefully evolving to meet modern tastes.

Our Heritage Timeline

Our Culinary Philosophy

At The Deccan Table, we believe in "slow cooking and fast serving". Our chefs still use the original charcoal tandoors and age-old marination techniques that made us famous.

We source ingredients daily from local markets, maintaining relationships with farmers and spice vendors that span decades. Every dish tells a story - whether it's the saffron in our biryani from Kashmir or the chili peppers from Guntur.

Family Legacy

Today, third-generation owner Zoya Ahmed continues the family tradition with her team of master chefs. "We're not just serving food," Zoya says, "we're keeping alive memories and traditions that make Indian cuisine so special."
